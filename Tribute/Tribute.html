Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India, was a leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. His philosophy of nonviolent resistance played a key role in India's struggle for freedom.

Gandhi led various campaigns for civil rights, social reforms, and the end of untouchability in India. His famous Salt March in 1930 and the philosophy of Satyagraha (nonviolent resistance) left a lasting impact on the world.

Key events in Mahatma Gandhi's life include the Champaran and Kheda Satyagrahas, the Non-Cooperation Movement, and the Quit India Movement. He was imprisoned several times for his activism but remained steadfast in his commitment to nonviolence.

Some of Mahatma Gandhi's notable quotes include:

"Be the change that you wish to see in the world."
"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others."
"An eye for an eye only ends up making the whole world blind."

Mahatma Gandhi's legacy extends beyond India's independence. His ideas of peace, tolerance, and harmony remain relevant globally, serving as a guide for those advocating for justice and equality.
